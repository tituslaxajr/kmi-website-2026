Our Story
Kapatid Ministry was founded in 2003 by Titus and Beth Laxa, who returned from working overseas in Malaysia to the Philippines with a mission to minister to the families of Overseas Filipino Workers (OFWs). Established to spread the Gospel among communities that had not heard it, Kapatid Ministry has since expanded its outreach, focusing on holistic transformation through spiritual guidance, addressing the needs of OFWs, out-of-school youth, and underprivileged children in marginalized communities across the Philippines.
The ministry operates with a theological goal, aiming to glorify God by transforming communities through the lived and preached Gospel. Kapatid Ministry partners with local churches to implement various programs, including Bible studies for OFW families, feeding programs for children, educational scholarships, and ministry to senior citizens. These initiatives are designed not only to share the message of salvation but also to demonstrate God’s love through practical acts of kindness, fostering a connection that ideally leads individuals into church communities or supports the planting of new churches.

Our Commitment
Financial Responsibility
The supporting organization or church or individual has the right to request information from Kapatid Ministry pertaining to ministerial doctrine, practices, finances, etc. Kapatid Ministry is to publish an annual report of activities and finances annually.

Spiritual Responsibility
Though Kapatid Ministry operates in partnership with various local churches belonging to a variety of denominations, we have ensured that our leaders and staff are under the authority and shepherding of the leaders of a local church.

Legal Responsibility
As a non-profit organization, Kapatid Ministry has a board of trustees to help in directing and monitoring its activities. This is to ensure that policies and procedures are done in compliance with local laws and regulations.
